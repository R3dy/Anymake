# Phase 1 — Discovery

**Goal:** Understand the product deeply before writing any code. Research, interviews, competitive analysis.

**Duration:** 1-3 sessions depending on complexity

## Steps

### Step 1.1 — Product Deep Dive

For the product defined in `PROJECT.md`, answer:
- Who exactly uses this?
- What do they do today to solve this problem?
- What's the simplest version of this product that actually works?
- What would make someone switch from their current solution?

### Step 1.2 — Competitive Landscape

Research:
- Direct competitors (solve same problem same way)
- Indirect competitors (solve same problem differently)
- Substitute solutions (people do nothing or use a workaround)

For each:
- What do they do well?
- What are their weaknesses?
- What can we learn from them?

### Step 1.3 — User Workflow Mapping

For the primary user:
- What does their current workflow look like?
- Where are the friction points?
- What would a better workflow look like?

Document as: **As a [user], I want to [action] so that [outcome]**

### Step 1.4 — Constraints & Risks

- Technical constraints (budget, timeline, expertise)
- Regulatory / legal considerations
- Technical risks (known unknowns, unknown unknowns)
- Assumptions we're making

### Step 1.5 — Success Metrics Refinement

Revisit Phase 0 success definition with new knowledge:
- Update user metrics if needed
- Add engagement metrics
- Define what "engagement" means for this product

## Output

- `docs/01-discovery.md` — comprehensive discovery document
- `docs/COMPETITORS.md` — competitive analysis
- Updated `PROJECT.md` with refined understanding
